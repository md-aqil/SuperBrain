Peripheral Vision

  • 1

    Believe You Can

    Emphasize the power of belief. Whether you believe you can or can't, you're right. It's about mind over matter and the importance of adopting a positive mindset.

  • 2

    Immediate Results with Techniques

    Highlight the idea of providing immediate results with techniques. By unlearning bad habits and applying new techniques, readers can experience early growth and improvement in their reading speed.

  • 3

    Peripheral Vision Technique

    Introduce the peripheral vision technique. Quick readers utilize their peripheral vision to see groups of words at once, reducing the number of stops and making reading less taxing.

  • 4

    Indentation Technique

    Explain the indentation technique, which can boost reading speed by up to 25%. By indenting about an inch on both sides with your finger, you can cover the text effectively while saving time and energy.

  • 5

    Interactive Learning

    Stress the importance of interactive learning. Mere listening won't bring results; readers need to experience and practice the techniques for better understanding and improvement.

  • 6

    Ten-Minute Practice

    Guide readers to grab their practice book and practice the indentation technique for three minutes. Emphasize that perfection is not the goal but enjoying the process and bringing a sense of play into the practice.

Your Actions for Today

  • Read for 10 minutes.
  • Keep scheduling your reading around times you are most alert and focused.
  • When you do your 10 minutes of reading, use your finger as a visual pacer.
